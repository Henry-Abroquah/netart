    • Digital Visions: Exploring the Intersection of Technology and Art

    Exhibition curated by Henry Abroquah

    The net art exhibition, "Digital Visions: Exploring the Intersection of Technology and Art," presents a collection of works that are intricately connected, establishing a cohesive narrative that extends beyond the confines of individual pieces. Each artwork not only stands on its own as a testament to the creative potential of the digital medium but also establishes a dialogue with the other works, creating a network of interconnected themes and ideas. The exhibition begins with Olia Lialina "My boyfriend came back from the war," a piece that utilizes fragmented storytelling and animated GIFs to depict the disorienting experience of a soldier's return home. This work sets the foundation for the exhibition's exploration of fragmented narratives and challenges the notion of linear storytelling. Building upon this theme, the duo JODI's "oss'' offers a satirical critique of open-source software, challenging the boundaries of collaboration and control within the digital landscape. This work establishes a connection to Lialina's piece by questioning the concept of narrative control and highlighting the complexities of digital authorship. From here, the exhibition shifts to Lialina's "Agatha Appears," an early example of net art that immerses viewers in a surreal digital landscape. This work expands upon the theme of fragmented narratives by challenging traditional notions of storytelling and inviting viewers to explore a nonlinear digital experience. It establishes a connection to "My boyfriend came back from the war" through their shared exploration of narrative fragmentation and the use of the digital medium to convey disorienting experiences. The connection continues with JODI's "Jodi %20 Wrong," a piece that disrupts conventional web design through code manipulation and glitch aesthetics. This work further challenges the boundaries of digital representation, connecting to the themes of disruption and unconventional narratives established by Lialina's works. Finally, Natalie Bookchin and Alexei Shulgin's "Introducing to Net Art.art" serves as a foundational piece that marks the inception of net art itself. It showcases the experimental spirit and innovative approaches of early net artists, establishing a connection to all the previous works in the exhibition by highlighting the shared exploration of the digital medium as a tool for artistic expression and cultural commentary. The net art exhibition "Digital Visions" presents a cohesive and interconnected narrative by carefully selected works that share thematic resonances.
